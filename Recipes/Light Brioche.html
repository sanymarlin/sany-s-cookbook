Light Brioche

Category : Pastry

Yield : 16 servings

Source : Sany

Ingredients

  • 500 g whole wheat flour
  • 100 g almond flour
  • 150 g fine semolina
  • 100 g honey
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp active dry yeast - or 2 packages
  • 3 large eggs
  • 250 ml milk - lukewarm
  • 80 g walnut oil

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the wheat flour, almond flour, semolina, honey, and salt. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in lukewarm milk and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy. Make a well in the dry ingredients and pour in the yeast mixture and eggs. Mix until the dough starts coming together.
  2. Incorporate the Walnut Oil: Gradually add the walnut oil while kneading. Knead for 10-15 minutes until the dough is smooth, elastic, and slightly sticky.
  3. First Rise (Bulk Fermentation): Cover the bowl with a cloth and let the dough rise in a warm place for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until doubled in size.
  4. Shaping & Second Rise: Punch down the dough and shape it into a loaf or small brioche rolls. Place in a greased loaf pan or on a baking sheet. Cover and let rise again for 45-60 minutes, until puffy.
  5. Baking: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Brush the brioche with a beaten egg for a shiny golden crust (optional). Bake for 30-35 minutes, until golden brown and firm.
  6. Cooling & Serving: Let cool before slicing to keep the texture intact. Enjoy warm or at room temperature with butter, jam, or honey!
