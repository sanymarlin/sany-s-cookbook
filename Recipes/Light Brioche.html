Light Brioche

Category: Pastry
Yield: 8 servings
Source: Sany
500 grams whole wheat flour
50 grams honey
1 teaspoon salt
10 grams active dry yeast - or 1 package
4 large eggs
125 ml milk - lukewarm or more?
50 gr walnut oil

Activate the Yeast: In a small bowl, mix the lukewarm almond milk with the active dry yeast and 1 tablespoon of honey. Let it sit for about 10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

Mix the Dry Ingredients: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.

Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Create a well in the center of the dry ingredients. Add the eggs, the rest of the honey, oil, and the yeast mixture. Mix until a rough dough forms.

Knead the Dough: Knead the dough for about 10-15 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. The dough may feel slightly sticky due to the honey and oil, but avoid adding too much extra flour.

First Rise: Form the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover with a clean cloth or plastic wrap. Let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

Shape the Brioche: After the first rise, punch down the dough to release the air. Shape it into a loaf and place it in a greased loaf pan, or divide it into small portions for buns.

Second Rise: Cover the shaped dough and let it rise again for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, until it has puffed up nicely.

Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes for a loaf, or 15-20 minutes for buns, until golden brown. The brioche should sound hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Cool and Serve: Allow the brioche to cool on a wire rack before slicing. Enjoy plain, with extra honey, or your favorite spread.
